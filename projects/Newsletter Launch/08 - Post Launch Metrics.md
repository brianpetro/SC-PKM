# Post Launch Metrics

The launch should be judged by usefulness signals, not only subscriber count. Useful early metrics include replies to the welcome email, saves or clicks on templates, direct comments from readers, and how many people forward an issue to another consultant.

Open rate can identify delivery problems, but it should not drive the editorial calendar by itself. A lower-open issue with strong replies may be more valuable than a high-open issue that nobody acts on.

Post-launch review should connect metrics to the first four issue calendar, reader feedback, and sponsor pitch notes. The goal is to learn which topics deserve repetition and which promises need to be made clearer.
