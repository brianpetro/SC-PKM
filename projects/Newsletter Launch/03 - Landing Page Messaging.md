# Landing Page Messaging

The landing page should describe the newsletter as a weekly field guide for consultants who want better clients and less scattered follow-up. The headline should be specific but calm: "A weekly system for finding better consulting work."

The supporting copy should explain what arrives each week: one practical idea, one real example, and one action. It should avoid promises about guaranteed revenue, because beta readers reacted badly to exaggerated marketing language.

The page needs a short section for who it is for, who it is not for, and what the first four issues will cover. The launch plan should connect this messaging to reader interviews, the first issue calendar, and objections from beta feedback.
