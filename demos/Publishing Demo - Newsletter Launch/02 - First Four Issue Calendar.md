# First Four Issue Calendar

The first four newsletter issues should prove the promise quickly. Issue one introduces the weekly operating rhythm. Issue two shows how to turn a messy client problem into a clear offer. Issue three covers follow-up emails after discovery calls. Issue four explains a Friday review that captures useful sales and delivery lessons.

Each issue should include a short story from a consultant, a practical template, and one action readers can complete before the next issue. The calendar should stay close to the reader interview themes so subscribers feel that the newsletter understands their actual work.

Avoid clever topic names during launch. Clear issue titles will make the archive easier to browse and will help Smart Connections link the calendar back to reader research, landing-page copy, and feedback notes.
