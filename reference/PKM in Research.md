> Apply PKM to capture sources, link arguments, and draft research outputs.

> [!When to use]
> If sources and arguments are scattered, this note shows how to capture, link, and draft research outputs from your notes, even with a pile of PDFs.
> Research notes feel scattered and citations go missing? Use this to run literature reviews, manage data, and collaborate with PKM, even when your sources live in five places.
> If quotes lose their provenance and you cannot cite cleanly later, this note shows how to keep citations traceable end-to-end, even if your tools change.

### Typical academic workflows PKM supports
- Literature review and citation management.
- Research design and method tracking.
- Data collection notes and traceability.

### Research capture pipeline from sources
- Research PKM starts with provenance: you must be able to trace claims back to sources.
- A basic pipeline: 1) Capture sources with enough metadata (title, author, link, date).
- 2) Create literature notes that preserve context.

### Literature review and annotation patterns
- One literature note per source with citation fields and contextual quotes.
- A topic MOC per research question that links claims to evidence.
- A "related work" synthesis note that groups papers by stance or method.

### What traceability means in research notes
- Where did this claim come from?
- What exactly did the source say (and in what context)?
- How confident am I, and what would change my mind?

### Citation metadata fields to capture
- Citation key (ex: Smith2022SemanticLinking)
- Title
- Author(s)

### Literature notes and citation practices
- Citation identifier (author-year or key).
- Where you found it.
- Key claims and evidence.

### Linking literature notes to sources cleanly
- One [[Literature Notes]] file that captures your reading and highlights.
- One source record (in a reference manager or a source note).
- Multiple permanent notes that cite the source where appropriate.

### Research data organization and traceability
- Keep a "data provenance" note: where the data came from, how it was cleaned, what versions exist.
- Link methods to datasets: "This method used dataset Y version Z".
- Record assumptions and exclusions explicitly.

### Link arguments and methods across papers
- Claim -> evidence notes (papers, datasets).
- Method -> where it works / fails.
- Contrasts and tensions between papers.

### Collaboration and sharing boundaries
- What can be shared publicly vs privately?
- How do you share notes without leaking personal commentary?
- How do you keep citations and provenance intact when collaborating?

### Workflow from reading to writing citations
- Save PDF/link and record the citation key.
- Write 2-3 questions you want the source to answer.
- Quotes with exact locations

### Turn findings into permanent concepts
- Extract one claim per note.
- Add enough context so it stands alone.
- Link to related concepts and methods.

### Drafting workflow from note clusters
- Drafting is easiest when you already have a cluster.
- Drafting workflow: 1) Choose a cluster of 5-15 concept notes.
- 2) Write a rough outline in a synthesis note.

### Common failure modes and fixes
- Fix: every quote should be paired with "why it matters" in your own words.
- Fix: capture citation metadata at the moment you create the literature note.
- Fix: capture fewer sources, distill more. Use [[Focus on Relevance]] and push toward synthesis.
- Fix: require a location reference for every quote.
- Fix: keep quotes and your writing visually distinct, and always link to the source note.
- Fix: choose one key format and enforce it during capture.
