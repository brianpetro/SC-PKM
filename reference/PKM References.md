> [!summary]
> Curated blogs, communities, and reference hubs for deepening PKM practice.

> [!When to use]
> If your reference list keeps growing but your skill does not, this note shows how to curate and consume PKM references without overload.

### High-signal sources and why they matter
- Templates and example vaults you can copy.
- Canonical books and frameworks (e.g., [[Building a Second Brain by Tiago Forte]], [[How to Take Smart Notes by Sonke Ahrens]]).
- Official documentation (tool docs, search syntax, plugin docs).

### How to consume references without overload
- Subscribe to fewer sources; read more deeply.
- Capture only what you can apply in the next 30 days.
- Prefer "one good source per problem" instead of 10 mediocre ones.

### Capture and distill reference material
- Capture references as usable assets.
- Pattern: 1) Create a note for the reference (book, article, tutorial).
- 2) Write: why you saved it, what job it helps with, and when you will use it.

### Community use for questions and patterns
- Solving a specific problem.
- Finding a proven pattern.
- Getting feedback on your system.

### Periodic pruning of your reference list
- Fix: convert links into notes with a next action, or delete them.
- Keep what you used.
- Archive what you did not touch.
