> [!summary]
> Turn personal insight into public value via writing, teaching, and collaboration.

> [!When to use]
> If your best ideas stay private and unused, this note shows how to turn notes into shareable value, without oversharing.

### Decide what is shareable and why
- Sharing because it feels productive, not because it serves an outcome. Fix: define the audience and the outcome first.
- Why share this? (teach, align, influence, build reputation, collaborate)
- Who is the audience? (team, peers, public)

### Formats (posts, talks, templates, workshops)
- Short post: one idea + one example.
- Long essay: argument + evidence + counterpoints.
- Talk: structure + stories + exercises.

### Prepare notes for reuse and audience clarity
- Publishing raw notes with no framing. Fix: write a short intro that states audience, problem, and payoff.
- Add context: what problem does this solve?
- Add examples: show how it works in reality.

### Consent, privacy, and redaction checks
- Accidental leaks through screenshots or attachments. Fix: treat attachments as part of the content to redact.
- Remove confidential names, numbers, and links.
- Avoid sharing private reflections that could harm you or others.

### Build a reusable publishing pipeline
- One-off publishing bursts that collapse. Fix: set a sustainable cadence and keep outputs small if needed.
- Capture ideas as seeds.
- Build a note cluster.
