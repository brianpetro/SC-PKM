> [!summary]
> A repeatable path from idea capture to draft to publish, with clear stage transitions.

> [!When to use]
> If drafts stall and you keep restarting, this note gives a pipeline from idea to publish so progress is predictable, without forcing daily writing.

### Pipeline stages from idea to published output
- A simple pipeline: 1) Seed: capture ideas and sources.
- 2) Cluster: gather related notes for one question.
- 3) Outline: create structure (claims/categories).

### Intake from inbox and note clusters
- Use [[Synthesis Workflow]] to turn the cluster into an outline.
- Inbox captures (ideas, links).
- Permanent notes that keep resurfacing.

### Drafting, editing, and review checkpoints
- Timebox the first draft (ex: 30-60 minutes).
- Write rough, then refine.
- Structure: does the outline still hold?

### Tracking work in progress and next steps
- One "Writing Pipeline" MOC (see [[MOC Writer]]) listing active pieces by stage.
- Each piece has a next action at the top.
- Keep WIP visible: Rule: limit active drafts.

### Reuse and republishing patterns
- Turn one evergreen note into a short internal memo.
- Combine 3 permanent notes into a talk outline.
- Republish by reframing: same ideas, different audience.
