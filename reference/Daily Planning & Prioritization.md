> [!summary]
> Daily planning is selecting a few outcomes and protecting time for them.

> [!When to use]
> When you start the day busy but end it unsure what mattered.

### The 3 outcomes rule
- Pick 1-3 outcomes.
- Each outcome should be a deliverable (something you can point to).
- Everything else is optional.

### How to pick the top 3
- Choose 1 high-leverage outcome (moves a project).
- Choose 1 maintenance outcome (keeps systems from breaking).
- Choose 1 small win (fast closure).

### Concrete example
1) Draft the Smart Start landing page outline.
2) Process inbox to under 20 items.
3) Reply to 3 stakeholder emails using templates.

### Failure mode and fix
Failure mode: You pick tasks, not outcomes ("answer emails").

Fix: rewrite as an outcome ("process inbox to zero in two windows"). Then schedule it (see [[Time Blocking]]).

### Covers / Does not cover
- Covers: A minimal daily planning method that fits in 5-10 minutes.
- Does not cover: Long-term goal design (see [[SMART Goals]] and [[OKRs (Objectives and Key Results)]]).
