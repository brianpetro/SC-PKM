> [!summary]
> Book notes on PARA and distillation to turn captured knowledge into action and output.

> [!When to use]
> You want the usable parts of BASB without rereading the book; this note pulls out the moves that work and flags where PARA can break, even if your life does not fit neat buckets.

### Core thesis and mental model
- Capture less, but capture with intent.
- Organize for action and reuse, not for perfect taxonomy.
- Distill so your future self can skim and act.

### PARA overview and why it works
- **Projects**: active deliverables with an end.
- **Areas**: ongoing responsibilities.
- **References**: reference material and reusable knowledge.

### Distillation and progressive summarization ideas
- Layer 0: raw capture.
- Layer 1: highlights (what seems important).
- Layer 2: bolding/marking the key lines.

### Implementation steps and constraints
- Avoid collecting without processing.
- Keep organizing lightweight.
- Distill only what you will actually reuse.

### Critique and when it breaks
- Use [[Focus on Relevance]] to filter inputs.
- References becomes a dumping ground (too much, too broad).
- You optimize for collecting and organizing, not output.
