> [!summary]
> A keystone habit is a small behavior that improves multiple other behaviors.

> [!When to use]
> When you want to improve many things but need one starting lever.

### How to identify a keystone habit
- It reduces friction in multiple areas.
- Missing it makes the day worse.
- Doing it makes other good behavior more likely.

### Common examples
- A weekly review.
- A daily planning pass.
- A short shutdown routine.

Your keystone habit depends on your context.

### Concrete example
Weekly keystone: [[PKM Weekly Review]]. When it happens, inbox stays small, projects stay current, and decisions are easier.

### Failure mode and fix
Failure mode: You pick a habit that is too big ("exercise 60 minutes daily").

Fix: shrink it to an identity-consistent minimum ("walk 10 minutes after lunch").
