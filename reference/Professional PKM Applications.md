> [!summary]
> Use PKM at work for projects, clients, decisions, collaboration, and reuse.

> [!When to use]
> If work notes disappear, duplicate, or become risky to share, this note shows how to use PKM professionally with real constraints, not theory.

### Work use cases and value levers
- Meeting prep and follow-through.
- Project and stakeholder context on demand.
- Decision rationale you can reuse.

### Client, project, and decision documentation patterns
- Project hub notes (scope, milestones, risks).
- Meeting notes that extract actions and decisions.
- Decision logs for "why we chose this".

### Collaboration and sharing boundaries
- Share a sanitized summary note, not raw meeting notes.
- Separate private reasoning from publishable outcomes.
- Use a "redaction pass" before sharing.

### Retrieval and reuse for speed and quality
- Use saved queries and predictable naming (see [[Efficient Retrieval]]).
- Pull the last decision log when the same tradeoff appears again.
- Reuse a template for recurring meetings.

### Compliance and privacy considerations
- Do not store secrets you cannot protect.
- Be careful with personal data and confidential client information.
- Ensure backups and access control match sensitivity.
