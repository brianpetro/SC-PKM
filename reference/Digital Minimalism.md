> [!summary]
> Digital minimalism is designing your digital life so it supports your values and work.

> [!When to use]
> When you feel pulled by feeds, notifications, and random apps more than your priorities.

### Principle
Keep the tools that clearly serve your goals, and remove or constrain the rest.

This is not about deprivation; it is about choosing defaults.

### A practical approach
1) Identify your top 2-3 priorities for the next 30 days.
2) List the digital tools that help those priorities.
3) Remove or constrain the tools that do not.
4) Replace the habit (do not leave a vacuum).

### Concrete example
Goal: write consistently.
- Keep: writing app, calendar, capture inbox.
- Constrain: social media to 1 window/day.
- Replace: when bored, open reading list or draft note instead.

### Failure mode and fix
Failure mode: You do a full detox, then rebound.

Fix: make one permanent default change per week (notifications, app limits, home screen). See [[Managing Distractions]].

### Covers / Does not cover
- Covers: Intentional tool selection and constraint patterns.
- Does not cover: Device-specific setup tutorials (use [[Focus Mode Setup Checklist]]).
