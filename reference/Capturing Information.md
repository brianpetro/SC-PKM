> [!summary]
> Capture fast with enough context to process later during review.

> [!When to use]
> If you capture too little (lose ideas) or too much (drown later), this note shows how to capture fast with just enough context, even on the go.
> When ideas vanish because capture is slow, this note helps you pick tools that make capture effortless, without locking your notes into one app.
> If your captures become a graveyard, this note shows how to keep an inbox that stays small and moving, even if you capture from anywhere.

### What to capture vs ignore
- Change a decision you will make.
- Become part of an output (a draft, a plan, a talk, a template).
- Save you time later (a reference you will reuse).

### Capture tool categories (text, web, audio)
- Desktop: Obsidian capture hotkey -> [[Capturing Information]].
- A single hotkey that opens a new note in your inbox.
- Mobile widget or shortcut that appends a line to an "inbox" note.

### Selection criteria (speed, portability, search)
- Speed: can you capture in under 10 seconds?
- Portability: can you export to plain text or markdown?
- Search: can you find captures before they are processed?

### Minimum context to include in captures
- What: the idea, quote, link, or observation.
- Why: why you saved it (the intended use).
- Source: where it came from (person, URL, book, meeting).
- What: the content.
- Why: intended use.
- Source: where it came from (optional but high leverage).

### Where captures go (inbox rules)
- Capture first. Do not organize in the moment.
- Keep captures short and low-friction.
- If the capture is a task, mark it as such so it does not get buried.

### What goes into the inbox and why
- Fleeting ideas you cannot fully write yet.
- Links and references you want to evaluate later.
- Meeting notes that need extraction (decisions/actions).

### Inbox routing and triage integration
- Every capture goes to one place first: [[Capturing Information]].
- Do not pre-file. Processing happens later (see [[Processing and Organizing Notes]]).
- Keep the inbox lightweight: text first, attachments only when necessary.

### Processing queues and states
- New: captured, unprocessed.
- In progress: partially processed, needs follow-up.
- Converted: upgraded into a note type (permanent, literature, decision).

### When and how captures get processed
- Daily: 5-15 minutes at end of day (or first coffee) to process the inbox.
- Weekly: 30-60 minutes to clean up leftovers and convert one cluster into output.
- Convert 1-3 captures into durable notes or next actions.

### Timeboxing and review triggers
- Daily: 5-15 minutes (process newest first).
- Weekly: 30-60 minutes (sweep leftovers, prune).
- End-of-day shutdown.

### Rules that keep inbox from becoming storage
- Inbox items expire unless upgraded (ex: 14 days).
- Do not create "inbox for each project". One inbox wins.
- If an item is not worth processing, delete it.

### Tool bloat risks and guardrails
- Failure mode: "I have 6 capture tools and none is consistent."
- Failure mode: "Captures get lost in email, DMs, and screenshots."
- Quarterly tool audit: delete or disable one tool.
