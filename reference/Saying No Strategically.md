> [!summary]
> A good no is a clear trade: what you protect, and what you are (not) committing to.

> [!When to use]
> When requests are pulling you away from priorities, or you keep saying yes and then resenting the commitment.

### Decision test
- What priority does this support?
- What gets displaced if I say yes? (see [[Priority Displacement Rule]])
- Is there a smaller commitment that would still help?
- Can I delegate, delay, or decline?

### Response patterns (short scripts)
- **No + reason (brief)**: "I cannot take this on this week; I am committed to X."
- **No + alternative**: "I cannot do the full request, but I can do Y."
- **Delay**: "I can revisit this on DATE; if it is urgent, it needs another owner."
- **Clarify**: "What is the outcome you need? I can support with the smallest version."

### Failure mode and fix
- Failure mode: you give vague answers that create limbo.
- Fix: be explicit about no/yes and the timeline.
- Failure mode: you say yes reflexively.
- Fix: add a pause rule: "I will confirm by tomorrow."

### Covers / Does not cover
- Covers: A decision test and clear response patterns for declining or renegotiating.
- Does not cover: HR/legal guidance for employment disputes.
