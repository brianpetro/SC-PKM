> [!summary]
> Index of inactive projects and archived references in a PARA-aligned system.

> [!When to use]
> If old material keeps distracting you, this note helps you park it safely in Archives so active work stays clear, without losing anything you might need later.

### Purpose and scope
- Archives is where inactive material goes to stay out of the way but remain recoverable.
- Archive finished projects, paused initiatives, and stale references; keep the link trail intact.
- Think 'cold storage you can search', not 'trash can'.

### How to use this MOC
- Move items here when they are not active, but you might need them for reference or compliance.
- Keep structure light: folders by year or area are fine, but do not over-organize.
- When you reactivate something, move it back to [[Projects]] or [[References]] and update the hub.

### Key links
- [[Future Proofing]] matters most for Archives because you only notice loss years later.
- [[Version Control for Notes]] gives you recoverability for long-lived archives.
- [[Centralized Repository]] keeps Archives searchable across devices.

### Maintenance and review
- Monthly: archive completed projects and close loops so active lists stay current.
- Yearly: export/back up Archives and prune anything you truly do not need.

### Common pitfalls
- Archiving without closure - add a final note on outcome and where to resume if needed.
- Using Archives as a junk drawer - if it has no plausible future use, delete it.
