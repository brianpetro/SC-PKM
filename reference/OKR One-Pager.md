> [!summary]
> OKRs work when the key results are measurable outcomes, not tasks.

> [!When to use]
> When you need a clear goal that can be tracked and reviewed weekly.

### Template
```
---
type: okr
cycle: "{{date:YYYY-[Q]Q}}"
owner: 
---

> [!summary]
> One objective with 3 measurable key results.

## Objective (1 sentence)
- 

## Why this matters
- 

## Key results (measurable outcomes)
- KR1:
- KR2:
- KR3:

## Weekly check-in rule
- Every week, update each KR with: current value, delta, next move.

## Risks / assumptions
-
```

### Failure mode and fix
Failure mode: Your key results are tasks ("launch landing page").

Fix: rewrite as outcomes ("conversion rate reaches X" or "N users complete onboarding").

### Covers / Does not cover
- Covers: A minimal OKR format and a weekly tracking rule.
- Does not cover: Company-wide OKR alignment processes.
