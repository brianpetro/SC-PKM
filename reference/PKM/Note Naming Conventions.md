> [!summary]
> Naming rules that make notes scannable, searchable, and safe to link.

> [!When to use]
> If titles are inconsistent and links feel risky, this note gives naming rules that keep notes findable and safe to rename, even as your vault evolves.

### Goals for good titles (findable, stable)
- Findable: the phrase you will actually search.
- Stable: you do not rename constantly.
- Unambiguous: one meaning in your vault.

### Recommended naming patterns by note type
- Definition/concept: "X" or "X definition" (only if you have collisions)
- Principle/standard: "How to X" or "X rules" or "X conventions"
- Method/workflow: "X workflow" or "How to do X"

### Rules for disambiguation and synonyms
- Add a qualifier: "PKM Inbox" vs "Email Inbox"
- Add domain: "Synthesis Workflow" vs "Synthesis Methods"
- Put synonyms in aliases, not in the title.

### Rename workflow and alias updates
- Safe rename workflow: 1) Rename the note title.
- 2) Add the old title as an alias (so search still works).
- 3) Update any hub/MOC notes that list it prominently.

### Common naming mistakes and fixes
- Fix: rewrite as a claim or job-to-be-done ("How to ...", "Why ...", "Checklist for ...").
- Fix: only rename when retrieval pain repeats 2+ times.
- Fix: invest in a better title and use [[Index Notes and MOCs]] to surface core notes.
