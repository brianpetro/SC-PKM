> [!summary]
> Templates reduce decision fatigue and keep email from turning into long thinking sessions.

> [!When to use]
> When email responses take longer than they should, or you keep rewriting the same messages.

### Templates
# Reply Templates

> [!summary]
> Use templates to reply fast and avoid open loops.

## 1) Acknowledge + set expectation
Subject: Re: {topic}

Thanks - I saw this. I will reply by {date}. If you need something sooner, tell me what is most urgent.

## 2) Quick no (clear + kind)
Thanks for thinking of me. I cannot take this on right now. If it helps, {alternative suggestion}.

## 3) Ask for clarification (reduce back-and-forth)
I can help, but I need 2 details:
1) What is the desired outcome?
2) When is it due?

## 4) Delegate (with owner + next step)
Looping in {name} who owns this. {name}, can you take the next step and reply with {specific deliverable} by {date}?

## 5) Close the loop
Done. Summary:
- {one bullet}
Next:
- {one bullet}

### Failure mode and fix
Failure mode: Templates sound robotic.

Fix: write them in your voice and keep only 5. Add one friendly sentence, then send.

### Covers / Does not cover
- Covers: Common email reply patterns.
- Does not cover: Negotiation strategy.

## Next action
- Copy one template into your email tool and use it for the next message you reply to.
