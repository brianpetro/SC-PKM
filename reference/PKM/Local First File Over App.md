> [!summary]
> Prefer plain files you control for portability, resilience, and long-term access.

> [!When to use]
> If you fear lock-in or losing access later, this note explains why local-first files win and how to keep convenience, without depending on one vendor.

### Why local-first reduces risk and lock-in
- the canonical copy of your notes is on a disk you control,
- in formats you can read without a specific app.
- Vendor shutdown or pricing changes.

### Preferred formats and folder conventions
- Markdown for notes.
- Standard media formats for attachments (PNG, JPG, PDF).
- Plain text exports for anything critical.

### Sync, backup, and conflict handling basics
- Sync helps you use notes on multiple devices.
- Backups protect you from deletion, corruption, or ransomware.
- Conflicts will happen; plan for them.

### Migration and export strategies
- Waiting until a tool breaks to think about migration. Fix: do small export drills before you need them.
- [[Version Control for Notes]] allows you to track changes and recover from mistakes during migrations.
- Keep filenames stable so links survive.

### Tradeoffs and where cloud-only still helps
- Local-first does not automatically mean safe. Apply [[Privacy and Security in PKM]] for threat modeling and hygiene.
- Cloud-only apps can be great for real-time collaboration.
- Some cloud features (sharing, web access) reduce friction.
