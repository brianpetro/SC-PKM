> [!summary]
> Tailor PKM to your goals and habits, then iterate based on real friction.

> [!When to use]
> If other people's workflows never quite fit, this note helps you design a PKM system around your goals and constraints, without endless tweaking.

### Identify your constraints and objectives
- Time available (5 min/day vs 60 min/day).
- Devices (desktop only vs mobile heavy).
- Sensitivity (private/work/confidential).

### Choose tools and structure that fit you
- PARA-first (see [[PARA Method]]).
- MOC-first (see [[Index Notes and MOCs]] and [[Linking Your Thinking]]).
- Hybrid: shallow folders + MOCs + a small tag canon.

### Design workflows that reduce friction
- Capture: where do ideas land (inbox rules)?
- Processing: when do you convert raw notes into usable notes?
- Review: what is your daily/weekly cadence?

### Diagnostic prompts to find weak points
- What do I keep failing to find later?
- What kind of notes keep piling up unprocessed?
- Where does my system feel slow or annoying?

### Iterate with small experiments and reviews
- Change one thing at a time.
- Timebox the experiment (1-2 weeks).
- Measure: did it reduce friction or improve output?
