> [!summary]
> Most outcomes come from a small subset of inputs; use that to choose where to focus.

> [!When to use]
> When you feel busy but results are flat, or you want to simplify by focusing on the highest leverage work.

### The idea (and the caveat)
- Roughly: 20% of inputs often produce 80% of outcomes.
- The exact numbers vary; the point is skew.
- Use it as a focusing heuristic, not a law.

### How to apply in your work
1) Pick one outcome (revenue, retention, learning, shipping).
2) List the last 10 actions that contributed.
3) Circle the top 2-3 that created most impact.
4) Cut or delegate one low-yield activity.
5) Protect time for the top actions (see [[Weekly Leverage Audit]]).

### Failure mode and fix
- Failure mode: you only optimize what is easy to measure.
- Fix: include qualitative signals (customer pain, quality, decision speed).
- Failure mode: you cut everything that is not immediate ROI.
- Fix: keep some exploration, but timebox it.

### Covers / Does not cover
- Covers: A leverage heuristic and a simple audit workflow.
- Does not cover: Detailed financial analysis or portfolio optimization.
