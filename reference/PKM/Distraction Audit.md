> [!summary]
> Distractions are often predictable. Fix the defaults, not your willpower.

> [!When to use]
> When you feel constantly interrupted or you keep context switching without noticing.

### Audit block
> [!summary]
> Identify your top distraction triggers and remove the default loops.

## Audit (10 minutes)
- List the top 5 times you got distracted yesterday:
	- What was I doing?
	- What pulled me away?
	- What did I do instead?

## Find your top 3 triggers
Common triggers:
- Notifications
- Open tabs
- Unclear next action
- Energy dip
- Avoiding a hard decision

## One change per trigger
Examples:
- Notifications -> disable all non-essential push alerts
- Open tabs -> close everything except the one you need; bookmark instead
- Unclear next action -> run [[Next Action Converter]]

## Proof metric (pick one)
- Distractions per hour (rough count)
- Screen time trend
- Completed focus blocks per week

### Failure mode and fix
Failure mode: You try to fix everything at once.

Fix: pick the top 1 trigger and remove one default loop this week.

### Covers / Does not cover
- Covers: Diagnosing distraction triggers and choosing one practical change.
- Does not cover: Long-term habit change programs.

## Next action
- Run the audit and choose one trigger to fix this week.
