> [!summary]
> The Eisenhower Matrix helps you decide what to do, schedule, delegate, or delete.

> [!When to use]
> When your task list is large and everything feels urgent, or you keep doing busywork instead of important outcomes.

### The 2x2
- **Important + Urgent**: do now.
- **Important + Not urgent**: schedule (protect with time blocks).
- **Not important + Urgent**: delegate or reduce.
- **Not important + Not urgent**: delete.

### How to apply in 10 minutes
1) List 10 tasks.
2) Mark importance (does it move a real outcome?).
3) Mark urgency (does it have a real deadline?).
4) Put each task in a quadrant.
5) Schedule the top 1-2 important/not-urgent tasks (see [[Daily Plan Template]]).

### Failure mode and fix
- Failure mode: you label everything important.
- Fix: define the outcome you are protecting this week, then re-classify.
- Failure mode: you never schedule important/not-urgent.
- Fix: block time first, then fit everything else around it.

### Covers / Does not cover
- Covers: A practical sorting method and decision rules for each quadrant.
- Does not cover: Long-term strategy prioritization across years.
