### GTD model overview (mind like water)
- Capture: get things out of your head.
- Clarify: decide what each item means.
- Organize: put it where you will see it at the right time.

### Capture everything into trusted system
- then moves through a processing step (see [[Processing and Organizing Notes]]).
- Capture fast, without judging.
- Capture in one trusted place (or one place per device that funnels to one place).

### Clarify into next actions and projects
- Is it actionable?
- If yes, what is the very next physical action?
- Is it a project (more than one step)?

## Overview of GTD
GTD is a time management method developed by David Allen. It focuses on capturing tasks and organizing them for effective execution.

### Capturing Tasks
In GTD, all tasks, ideas, and projects are captured in an external system, freeing mental space for execution.

### Organizing Tasks
Tasks are categorized by their next actions and context, such as calls to make or emails to send.

### Reviewing Tasks
Frequent reviews ensure tasks are up to date and that nothing falls through the cracks. Weekly reviews are a key part of the system.

### Executing Tasks
With a clear next action for each task, GTD allows for more efficient execution and reduced procrastination.
