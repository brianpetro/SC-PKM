> [!When to use]
> If reviews feel like chores, this note shows how to turn review time into insight and course-correction, even if you hate journaling.

### Review goals (clarity, memory, alignment)
- Clarity: reduce ambiguity and open loops.
- Memory: capture what mattered before it fades.
- Alignment: ensure actions match priorities.

### Reflection prompts to use consistently
- What mattered today?
- What surprised me?
- What do I want to remember?

### Turning review into refactoring actions
- Rename a note so it is findable.
- Split a note that holds multiple ideas.
- Add a relationship sentence and one link.

### Synthesis moments and outputs from review
- A paragraph that connects 3 notes into a new claim.
- A one-page playbook from repeated meeting outcomes.
- A decision record capturing a recurring tradeoff.

### Avoid review becoming busywork
- Fix: end the review with a visible artifact (one updated note, one decision, one draft paragraph).
- Fix: timebox refactoring to 10-15 minutes unless it directly enables an output.
- Fix: attach review to recurring moments (shutdown routine, weekly reset), which is the point of [[Regular Reviews]].

Reflection turns passive notes into personal insight. It helps you see patterns, refine thinking, and make better decisions.

### 🧠 Why It Matters
- Identifies what’s worth keeping
- Surfaces missed opportunities
- Builds a sense of growth over time

### 🛠️ How to Reflect in PKM
1. Use a daily or weekly rhythm ([[PKM Daily Review]], [[Regular Reviews]])
2. Ask reflection prompts:
- What did I learn?
- What’s unclear?
- What’s connected?
3. Add short reflections to the top of a note or as a follow-up
