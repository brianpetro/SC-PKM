> [!summary]
> Turn notes into deliverables: writing, presentations, decisions, and reusable artifacts.

> [!When to use]
> If your vault feels like a library instead of a workshop, this note shows how to turn notes into deliverables, even when you have limited time.

### Why output is the point of PKM
- Capture prevents loss.
- Organization reduces friction.
- Retrieval saves time.

### Types of output (docs, talks, decisions)
- Writing: essays, memos, research summaries, documentation.
- Presentations: talk outlines, slide structure, speaker notes.
- Decisions: decision logs, tradeoff analyses, ADR-style records.

### Workflow from notes to draft to ship
- Use [[Synthesis Workflow]] and [[Synthesis Workflow]] to extract claims, tensions, and an outline.
- Define the audience and "done".
- Collect 5-15 relevant notes and sources.

### Scheduling and constraints for consistency
- a stable pipeline (see [[Writing Pipeline]]),
- Timebox: ex: 30 minutes per day or 2x 90-minute blocks per week.
- WIP limit: only 1-2 active drafts at a time.

### Quality checks and reuse patterns
- Shipping output that is disconnected from the vault. Fix: link the output to the note cluster so it becomes part of your knowledge graph.
- Traceability: link key claims to notes or sources.
- Clarity: add definitions for key terms.
