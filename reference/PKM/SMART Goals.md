> [!summary]
> SMART turns wishes into goals you can act on and review.

> [!When to use]
> When a goal feels inspiring but you cannot tell what to do next, or whether you are making progress.

### SMART breakdown
- **Specific**: what exactly will be true?
- **Measurable**: how will you know? (metric or observable proof)
- **Achievable**: plausible with your constraints
- **Relevant**: supports current priorities (see [[Focus on Relevance]])
- **Time-bound**: by when?

Example rewrite:
- Vague: "Get healthier"
- SMART: "Walk 8,000 steps 5 days/week for 6 weeks and log it."

### Common mistakes and fixes
- Mistake: making it measurable with a vanity metric.
	- Fix: choose a metric that actually changes decisions.
- Mistake: turning the goal into a task list.
	- Fix: keep the goal as an outcome; tasks live elsewhere.
- Mistake: no review cadence.
	- Fix: schedule a weekly check-in (see [[Weekly KR Check-in]] for a pattern).
