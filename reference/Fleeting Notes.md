> [!summary]
> Fast, disposable captures meant to be processed or discarded, not stored forever.

> [!When to use]
> If quick captures are turning into permanent clutter, this note shows how to use fleeting notes as fuel you process or toss, even if you capture constantly.

### What fleeting notes are for (speed)
- You have an insight and cannot write a full note.
- You are in a meeting and need to capture fast.
- You are walking and want to record something before it disappears.

### Minimum structure to include in a fleeting note
- The idea in your words (1-3 lines).
- A "why" line (the intended use).
- A source pointer if relevant (person, link, book).

### Processing rules (convert, task, discard)
- A task (if it is an action).
- A permanent note (if it is a reusable idea).
- A reference (if it is a link you will need later).

### Timing and triggers for processing
- Daily: during [[PKM Daily Review]], process fleeting notes first.
- Weekly: sweep any leftovers and either convert or delete.
- Timeboxes and triggers: A good trigger: "end of day shutdown" - 10 minutes to empty the inbox.

### Anti-patterns like hoarding fleeting notes
- Failure mode: "I keep fleeting notes forever."
- Failure mode: "My fleeting notes are cryptic."
- Failure mode: "Fleeting notes become permanent without rewriting."
