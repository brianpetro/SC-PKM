> [!summary]
> Atomic, linkable notes written in your own words that survive beyond the source.

> [!When to use]
> If your notes never become something you can reuse, this note shows what makes a permanent note valuable and how to write one, without perfectionism.

### What qualifies as a permanent note
- Includes at least one example.
- Contains one main idea (atomic).
- Is understandable without the original source.

### Writing in your own words with context
- One concrete example.
- "An inbox is a staging area, not storage. It prevents capture from becoming filing. Example: a meeting note lands in inbox, then becomes a decision log."
- A short definition (what it is).

### Linking to related concepts and arguments
- Add 1-3 links that represent true relationships.
- Use relationship sentences (supports, contrasts, depends-on).
- Prefer linking to stable concept notes and workflows.

### Quality checks before marking permanent
- Example: does it include a concrete example?
- Title: would I search this phrase?
- Scope: is it one idea?

### How permanent notes power long-term output
- speed up writing by providing ready paragraphs,
- reduce rework by capturing reasoning once,
- build a reusable map of your thinking over time.
