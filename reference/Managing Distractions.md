> [!summary]
> Distraction management is designing your environment so focus is the default.

> [!When to use]
> When you lose time to pings, tab-hopping, or compulsive checking, even when you know what to do.

### The 3 levers
- **Notifications**: reduce what can interrupt you.
- **Environment**: reduce what can tempt you.
- **Boundaries**: reduce what can enter your day.

### High-leverage defaults
- Disable non-essential notifications.
- Put your phone in another room during deep work.
- Use one capture inbox so new inputs do not become instant context switches (see [[Capturing Information]]).

### Concrete example
Deep work block rule: "No messaging apps open. Phone out of reach. Only 1 browser window."

### Failure mode and fix
Failure mode: You rely on willpower, then fail on tired days.

Fix: make the environment do the work. Use [[Focus Mode Setup Checklist]] once, then you benefit every day.
