> [!summary]
> Calendar management is keeping your schedule honest so plans survive contact with reality.

> [!When to use]
> When your calendar looks full but important work still does not get done.

### 3 rules that keep calendars usable
- **Buffers** after meetings.
- **Labels** that tell you what the block is for.
- **Weekly reset** to reconcile commitments.

### Buffers that prevent chaos
- After any meeting block, add 10-30 minutes to capture decisions/actions and reset context.
- For high-stakes meetings, add prep blocks.

### Calendar labels that reduce ambiguity
Use a small, consistent label set. Example:
- Deep: outcome work
- Admin: maintenance
- Meeting: coordination
- Buffer: processing

See [[Calendar Block Labels]].

### Failure mode and fix
Failure mode: You schedule only meetings, then try to squeeze work into gaps.

Fix: place your deep blocks first, then fit meetings around them (see [[Time Blocking]]).

### Covers / Does not cover
- Covers: Practical scheduling rules, not just theory.
- Does not cover: Negotiation of meeting culture (see [[Saying No Strategically]]).
