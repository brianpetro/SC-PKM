> [!summary]
> Combine tools and methods into one coherent PKM workflow.

> [!When to use]
> If your tools do not work together, this note helps you design one coherent workflow, without creating fragile dependencies.

### Identify core jobs your tools must do
- Capture (fast, low friction).
- Store (durable, portable).
- Retrieve (search, navigation, queries).

### Integration points (capture, search, drafts)
- Capture -> inbox (where everything lands).
- Inbox -> processed note types (fleeting, literature, permanent).
- Notes -> drafts (outline, draft, revise).

### Automation and shortcut opportunities
- Fix: keep a review loop with acceptance rules.
- One-click saved searches (see [[Queries and Saved Searches]]).
- Hotkeys for capture and templates.

### Evaluate tools by portability and resilience
- Portability: can you export your data in plain formats?
- Resilience: what breaks when the tool disappears?
- Keep core notes in Markdown.

### Avoid tool bloat and conflicting workflows
- Fix: choose one source of truth and migrate or delete the rest.
- One primary editor/workspace (this vault).
- One primary inbox route.
