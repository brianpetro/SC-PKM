> [!summary]
> Reduce overwhelm by filtering inputs, batching processing, and archiving noise.

> [!When to use]
> When inputs keep piling up and you feel behind, this note helps you filter, batch, and archive so your vault stays high-signal, even if your inbox is already big.

### Diagnose overload sources and symptoms
- You save 40 articles in a week. On Friday you cannot remember why any of them mattered, so they sit forever. The fix is to add a capture boundary plus a weekly processing slot tied to an output goal.
- Your inbox grows faster than you process it.
- You keep clipping content but rarely retrieve it later.

### Input filters and capture boundaries
- Intent line required: every capture must include one line like "Saved because: ____".
- Rate limit: ex: max 5 captures per day from social, max 2 newsletters per week.
- Stop list: explicitly block inputs that never produce output for you.

### Batch processing and review cadence
- Daily (5-15 min): run [[PKM Daily Review]] to triage, extract actions, and add 1-3 links.
- Weekly (30-60 min): reset the inbox, convert a few items into durable notes, and choose one micro-output.
- Monthly (60-90 min): prune, archive, and adjust filters.

### Distill vs archive decision rules
- You can attach at least one example from your context.
- The idea is likely reusable across projects.
- You can state the core claim in your own words.

### Detox and maintenance routines
- Inbox zero sprint: 30 minutes per day for 5 days, process until inbox is under 20 items.
- No-capture day: once per week, forbid new intake and only distill or synthesize.
- Source pruning: unsubscribe from 3 sources that create noise.
