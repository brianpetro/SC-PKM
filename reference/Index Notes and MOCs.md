> [!summary]
> Navigation hubs that link out without duplicating content, keeping the vault browsable.

> [!When to use]
> If your vault is hard to browse, this note shows how MOCs create navigation without duplicating content, even in a small vault.

### Purpose of index notes and MOCs
- Create a stable entry point into a domain or project.
- See what you know at a glance.
- Build reading paths for onboarding and learning.
- Surface gaps ("what is missing?").

### Structure patterns (topic, project, timeline)
- Topic MOC: a map for a domain (ex: "PKM Foundations").
- Project MOC: links to decisions, meetings, and artifacts for one project.
- Timeline MOC: links grouped by week/month for history and review.
- Skill MOC: progressive path (beginner -> intermediate -> advanced).

### Rules to avoid duplicating atomic content
- Link out; do not rewrite the atomic notes.
- Add only a small amount of glue text:
- one sentence explaining why a link belongs.
- Keep lists short and curated.

### Maintenance and pruning routines
- Failure mode: "My MOC is stale."
- Failure mode: "My MOC became a mega-note."
- Remove broken or obsolete links.
- Merge duplicates.
