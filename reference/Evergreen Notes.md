> [!summary]
> Notes you keep improving over time because they stay useful and shape outputs.

> [!When to use]
> If good notes go stale and stop helping, this note shows how to keep a few notes improving over time, even if you only polish one a week.

### Definition and criteria for evergreen notes
- Durable: they stay relevant across projects.
- Clear: they can be understood without the original source.
- Linked: they have a few high-signal relationships.

### Update loop (review, refine, relink)
- A simple maintenance loop: 1) Review the note during a scheduled review (see [[Regular Reviews]]).
- 2) Refine the wording so the core claim is clear.
- 3) Add one new example or counterexample.

### How evergreen notes feed outputs
- becoming paragraphs in drafts,
- turning into checklists and standards,
- serving as stable references in decision memos.

### Signals a note should become evergreen
- You have referenced it in multiple contexts.
- You keep rewriting the same explanation elsewhere.
- The note is a recurring decision rule (ex: "what to capture").

### Avoid perfectionism and over-editing
- Failure mode: "I keep polishing and never ship."
- Failure mode: "I make everything evergreen."
- Failure mode: "Evergreen notes go stale."
