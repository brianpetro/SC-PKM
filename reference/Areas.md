> [!summary]
> Index of ongoing responsibilities (no end date) in a PARA-aligned system.

> [!When to use]
> When ongoing responsibilities blur together, this note helps you define Areas so you can maintain what matters, even if you have a lot of moving parts.

### Purpose and scope
- An Area is an ongoing responsibility you want to keep at a standard (health, finances, team, home).
- If there is no finish line, it belongs here; projects are the time-bound efforts inside an area.
- Areas work best as dashboards that point to the few notes you actually use.

### How to use this MOC
- List your Areas as wikilinks; keep one short section per Area with the essentials (metrics, routines, key docs).
- Link each Area to the projects currently supporting it (from [[Projects]]) and any recurring reviews.
- Move abandoned responsibilities to [[Archives]] instead of keeping zombie areas.

### Key links
- [[Regular Reviews]] keeps Areas alive so they do not silently decay.
- [[Time Management]] connects Area routines to your calendar and planning.
- [[Privacy and Security in PKM]] is worth revisiting for any Area that contains sensitive work.

### Maintenance and review
- Weekly: check the 1-2 Areas that most affect your week; adjust routines or next actions.
- Quarterly: prune the list; merge or archive Areas you are not actually maintaining.

### Common pitfalls
- Treating Areas like storage - keep links to current docs, not a pile of misc notes.
- Over-tracking - pick 1 metric or question per Area that signals health.
