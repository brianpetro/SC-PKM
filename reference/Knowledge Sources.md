> [!summary]
> A mix of books, articles, podcasts, and experience captured with context and intent.

> [!When to use]
> If you capture sources but lose why they mattered, this note shows how to keep provenance and intent so sources become usable later, even when you skim fast.

### Source types and their capture patterns
- Books: highlight + chapter summaries + concept extraction.
- Articles/blogs: capture the thesis and 3-5 key claims.
- Podcasts/talks: timestamped notes + takeaways.

### How to preserve provenance and context
- Copy-paste dumps with no framing. Fix: add a one-sentence thesis and your reason for saving it.
- Who/what (author, speaker, org).
- Where (URL, book title, venue).

### Quality filters and relevance checks
- Relevance: does it support current priorities (see [[Focus on Relevance]])?
- Credibility: is this source trustworthy enough for the claim?
- Novelty: is it adding something you do not already have?

### Distillation pipeline for sources
- A resilient pipeline: 1) Capture: store the source link/PDF and intent.
- 2) Extract: highlight and mark key lines.
- 3) Summarize: write your own short summary.

### Turn sources into linked insights and outputs
- Keeping sources isolated (a pile of PDFs) with no concept notes. Fix: require at least one distilled takeaway for any source you keep.
- Extract 1-3 atomic claims and write them in your own words.
- Link each claim to related concepts and projects.
