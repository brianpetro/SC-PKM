> [!summary]
> Time blocking is choosing when the work happens, not just what the work is.

> [!When to use]
> When your to-do list grows but the day fills with meetings and reactive work.

### Core idea
- A calendar is a constraint system.
- If something matters, it gets time.
- Blocks should be named by outcome, not activity.

### A minimal time-block plan
1) Pick 1-3 outcomes for the day.
2) Place the hardest one first (or in your best energy window).
3) Add buffers after meetings.
4) Add a shutdown block to process and reset.

### Concrete example
09:00-09:50 Draft onboarding section 1
10:00-10:30 Meeting
10:30-10:45 Buffer: extract actions/decisions
14:00-14:50 Build example vault artifact
17:10-17:25 Shutdown: inbox + next day top 3

### Failure mode and fix
Failure mode: You overbook blocks, then the plan breaks and you abandon it.

Fix: plan at 60-70% capacity and include buffers (see [[Scheduling & Calendar Management]]).
