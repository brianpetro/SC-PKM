### Survey of major note-taking methods
- **Outline method**: hierarchical bullets; best for structured lectures and talks.
- **Cornell method**: cues/questions + notes + summary; best for review and exam prep.
- **Mapping**: mind maps and concept maps; best for exploring relationships.

### When each technique fits best
- If the material is relational (theory, systems): mapping (see [[Mind Mapping]]).
- If the material is linear (lecture, meeting): outline or Cornell.
- If you are comparing: charting (matrix).

- Create prompts and schedule review (see [[Spaced Repetition]]).
- Write a 3-bullet summary from memory.
- Ask yourself questions and answer without looking.

### Technique pitfalls and quick improvements
- Fix: capture structure and key claims, not transcripts.
- Fix: always record why a highlight mattered to you.
- Fix: set a processing timebox and treat raw notes as perishable (see [[Fleeting Notes]]).
