> [!summary]
> Make notes findable with naming, tags, links, indexes, and smart search.

> [!When to use]
> You know you wrote it somewhere, but you cannot find it when it matters; this note shows how to make notes findable without over-engineering.

### Retrieval problems to solve (fast + accurate)
- You cannot remember the note exists.
- You cannot guess the title you used.
- The note exists but the content is too vague to trust.

### Naming, tagging, and linking as breadcrumbs
- Titles: primary retrieval handle (see [[Note Naming Conventions]]).
- Aliases: common alternate searches (acronyms, synonyms).
- Tags: grouping and queues, not meaning (see [[Tag Canon]]).

### Index notes and saved queries as shortcuts
- Index notes / MOCs: curated navigation for a topic or project (see [[Index Notes and MOCs]]).
- Saved searches: automated queues for review and action (see [[Queries and Saved Searches]]).
- MOC: you want a reading path or a curated map.

### Search tactics and query patterns
- Find by tag:
- `tag:#inbox`
- `tag:#workflow`

### Maintenance to prevent entropy
- Failure mode: "Titles drift and I cannot guess them."
- Failure mode: "Tags explode."
- Failure mode: "Links become spam."
