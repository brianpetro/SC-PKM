> [!summary]
> Habits stick when you make the behavior easy to start and rewarding to repeat.

> [!When to use]
> When you want consistency but motivation keeps fluctuating.

### The habit loop in plain language
- **Cue**: the trigger.
- **Routine**: the behavior.
- **Reward**: the payoff your brain learns.

### Make the habit easy to start
- Reduce the first step to 30-60 seconds.
- Prepare the environment ahead of time.
- Attach it to an existing anchor ("after coffee...").

### Concrete example
Habit: daily inbox processing.
Cue: after lunch.
Routine: process 3 inbox items.
Reward: mark the review as done and enjoy a short break.

### Failure mode and fix
Failure mode: You pick too many habits at once.

Fix: track 1-3 habits max and pick one that cascades (see [[Keystone Habits]]).
