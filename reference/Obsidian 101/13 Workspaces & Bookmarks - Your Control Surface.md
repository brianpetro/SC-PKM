Your goal: eliminate UI friction so Obsidian becomes *one keystroke away* from the next useful action.

This note creates a "control surface":
- **Workspaces** = saved layouts (Learn vs Write vs Review)
- **Bookmarks** = saved entry points (files + headings + searches)

---

## Part 1: Workspaces (save your learning layout)
### Why this is high leverage
If your panes aren't right, you'll avoid the tool.
A Workspace turns "setup overhead" into a one-time cost.

### Do this
- [ ] Open any lesson note (start with [[00 Start Here - Obsidian Quickstart Map]])
- [ ] Right sidebar: open **Backlinks** and **Outline**
- [ ] Left sidebar: keep **File explorer** and **Search** available
- [ ] Enable the **Workspaces** core plugin (Settings → Core plugins)
- [ ] Save workspace as: **Learn - Obsidian 101**

> [!tip]
> A good learning layout keeps "incoming context" visible:
> Backlinks answers: "Where is this used?"
> Outline answers: "Where am I in this note?"

## Part 3: Use Bookmarks + Search as dashboards (not as reference)
### The mental model
- Notes are *storage*
- Bookmarks are *entry points*
- Search is *navigation + filtering*
- Properties are your *schema*

### A simple routine (2 minutes)
- [ ] Open **Learn - Obsidian 101** workspace
- [ ] Click Start Here
- [ ] Run one saved query (tasks or active notes)
- [ ] Do one drill (link, embed, cleanup)

---

## Failure modes (and fixes)
- "I forget where things are"
	- Fix: bookmark the entry points; stop browsing folders.
- "My notes are messy"
	- Fix: do the 3-minute cleanup pass from [[12 Hotkeys]] immediately after capture.
- "I don't see connections"
	- Fix: keep Backlinks open while writing and add 1–2 links per note.
