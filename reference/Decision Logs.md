> [!summary]
> Record decisions with context, options, and rationale so future you can reuse the thinking.

> [!When to use]
> If you keep revisiting old decisions because the why is forgotten, this note shows how to log decisions so future-you can reuse the reasoning, even when you are moving fast.

### Decision record template fields
- Decision: what you decided, in one sentence.
- Date: when it was decided.
- Owner: who is accountable.

### Capture context, constraints, and options
- Writing decisions after the fact. Fix: create the log during the meeting or immediately after while the tradeoffs are still fresh.
- Constraints (time, budget, compatibility, privacy).
- Non-goals (what you are explicitly not optimizing).

### Link to evidence, meetings, and projects
- The meeting where it was discussed (use [[Meeting Notes Workflow]]).
- The project hub where it matters (use [[PKM in Project Management]]).
- The evidence notes or sources that informed it.

### Review decisions and outcomes later
- 2 weeks: check implementation and early signals.
- 6-8 weeks: compare expected vs actual outcomes.
- End of project: capture what you would do differently next time.

### Avoid hindsight bias and missing rationale
- Capturing only the conclusion ("we decided X") with no reasoning. Fix: always record at least one tradeoff sentence and one rejected option.
- Write the key assumptions at the time.
- Write what would falsify the decision.
