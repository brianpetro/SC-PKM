> [!summary]
> Procrastination often comes from unclear next steps or a task that feels too big to start.

> [!When to use]
> When you keep thinking about a task but cannot start, even though you have time.

### Two common causes
- **Ambiguity**: you do not know the first move.
- **Overwhelm**: the task is too large, so your brain avoids it.

### The fix in one line
Turn "work on X" into a next physical action you could do in 2-5 minutes.

Use [[Next Action Converter]].

### 5-minute starter sprint
Set a 5-minute timer.
- Only goal: complete the first tiny step.
- When the timer ends, either stop (you still won) or keep going because momentum exists.

See [[Starter Sprint]].

### Failure mode and fix
Failure mode: You use planning as procrastination.

Fix: timebox planning to 2 minutes, then start the 5-minute sprint.
