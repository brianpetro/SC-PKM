> [!summary]
> Distill sources into skimmable layers so notes become reusable.

> [!When to use]
> If highlights look meaningless later, this note shows how to distill sources in layers so your future self can skim and reuse them, even with limited time.

### Layer model for distilling information
- Layer 0: raw capture (source link/PDF + intent).
- Layer 1: highlights (what stands out).
- Layer 2: key lines (the few lines you want future you to see first).

### How to mark highlights and key lines
- Highlighting everything "just in case". Fix: force yourself to pick only 5 key lines.
- Highlight less than you think.
- Prefer claims, definitions, and examples over filler.

### Convert highlights into your words
- If a highlight is important, write it in your own words and attach one example from your context.
- Your note: "Reviews prevent entropy because they create a recurring chance to prune and reconnect. Example: weekly review clears the inbox and updates project hubs."
- What is the author really claiming?

### When to stop distilling and move to synthesis
- Endless distillation as procrastination. Fix: timebox summarization and require one micro-output.
- [[Synthesis Workflow]] to combine multiple notes into a new artifact.
- You can explain the idea without the source open.

### Avoid over-highlighting and losing meaning
- Saving sources without distilling them. Fix: "no distillation, no storage": archive it if you will not process it.
- If your source intake is high, use [[Avoiding Information Overload]] to reduce inputs and protect processing time.
- Keep a hard limit on key lines.
