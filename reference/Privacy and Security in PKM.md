> [!summary]
> Protect sensitive notes with access control, encryption options, and reliable backups.

> [!When to use]
> If you are worried about sensitive notes, this note helps you think clearly about risks and protections, even if you still need to share some work.

### Threat model and sensitive data categories
- Lost or stolen device.
- Misconfigured sync/sharing.
- Accidental publishing.

### Access control and encryption options
- Device security (strong passcode, automatic lock).
- Full-disk encryption (so lost devices do not expose files).
- Separate vaults or folders for sensitive content.

### Backup and recovery practices
- Test restores periodically (see [[Future Proofing]]).
- Encrypt backups if they contain sensitive notes.
- Keep backup keys safe and documented.

### Safe sharing and redaction workflows
- Copy notes into a "share" staging folder.
- Redact sensitive details (names, numbers, internal links).
- Remove attachments that reveal more than intended.

### Hygiene rules to reduce accidental leaks
- Keep a "never store" list (credentials, private IDs).
- Use consistent prefixes/tags for confidential notes.
- Avoid embedding secrets in screenshots.
