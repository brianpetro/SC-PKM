> [!summary]
> Keep the vault high-signal by aligning capture and organization to goals.

> [!When to use]
> When everything feels worth saving, your vault turns noisy; this note helps you filter by goals so what you keep actually supports output, even with many interests.

### Define goals and current priorities clearly
- Current priorities (next 2-6 weeks): what you are actively building or deciding.
- Persistent areas (always-on): health, relationships, career craft, finances, etc.
- Long-term themes: topics you want to compound over years.

### Capture filters and relevance heuristics
- Filtering by what is easy instead of what is important. The fix is to tie filters to outcomes, not convenience.
- Output test: will this help me produce an output in 30 days?
- Decision test: will this help me decide something real soon?

### Review practices to prune and refocus
- Weekly prune: archive low-value captures, delete duplicates.
- Monthly theme scan: what did you learn that actually mattered?
- Quarterly tag consolidation: remove one-off tags.

### Promote notes that enable near-term output
- Keeping everything at the same "raw" level. The fix is to choose 1-3 notes per week to upgrade into durable assets.
- It contains a reusable claim.
- It has a clear audience (future you, team, public).

### Avoid rabbit holes and stale collections
- Timebox research sessions.
- Require a draft before more sources.
- Keep a "not now" list to park tempting topics.
