> [!summary]
> A leverage audit turns 'busy' into a clearer set of outcomes and cuts.

> [!When to use]
> During weekly planning, when everything feels important and you need to choose.

### 15-minute audit checklist
- [ ] List the top 10 things you did last week (work + personal).
- [ ] Circle the 2 that created the most value.
- [ ] Identify 1 task you can delete.
- [ ] Identify 1 task you can delegate.
- [ ] Identify 1 task you can defer.
- [ ] Schedule 1 block for the highest leverage outcome next week.

### Concrete example
High leverage: "Draft onboarding email sequence".
Delete: "Reformat notes perfectly".
Delegate: "Compile competitor list".
Defer: "Optional reading backlog".

### Failure mode and fix
Failure mode: You keep everything and call it a plan.

Fix: the audit is not complete until you cut at least one thing.

### Covers / Does not cover
- Covers: Weekly prioritization via leverage and pruning.
- Does not cover: Long-term goal design (see [[OKRs (Objectives and Key Results)]]).
