> [!summary]
> Link-based thinking using bidirectional links and graph views to surface insight.

> [!When to use]
> If your notes are isolated and do not compound, this note shows how networked thought emerges from small links and refactors, without building a perfect graph.

### Definition of networked thought in PKM
- you write notes as nodes in a network,
- and you intentionally create edges (links) that represent meaning.
- notes stand alone,

### Bidirectional links and why they matter
- make connections discoverable from either side,
- allow accidental rediscovery,
- and create trails of thought you can follow later.

### Graph and cluster behaviors to look for
- Clusters: a set of notes that repeatedly co-occur around a topic.
- Bridges: notes that connect two clusters (often the source of new insight).
- Hubs: foundational concepts that many notes depend on.

### How to keep the network meaningful (context)
- Treating linking as a numbers game. Fix: cap links per note and require relationship sentences for key links.
- context: why a link exists,
- semantics: what the relationship is,

### Avoid shallow networks and over-linking
- Use LYT-style navigation via [[Linking Your Thinking]] so the network is usable, not just dense.
- Many links, few relationship sentences.
- Notes that are vague and could link to anything.
