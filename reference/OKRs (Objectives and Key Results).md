> [!summary]
> OKRs separate the outcome you want from the measures that prove it.

> [!When to use]
> When you need clarity and alignment on what success means, especially across a team or a time-boxed period.

### Core model
- **Objective**: a qualitative outcome (what you want to achieve).
- **Key Results**: 2-5 measurable outcomes that prove the objective.

Good pattern:
- Objective: "Make onboarding fast and reliable"
- KR1: "Reduce time-to-first-success from 30 min to 10 min"
- KR2: "Increase activation rate from X% to Y%"

### Good vs bad key results
- Good KR: measurable outcome (metric moves).
- Bad KR: a task list ("launch feature", "write docs").
- Fix: turn tasks into hypotheses that should move an outcome metric.

### Cadence
- Set OKRs for a fixed window (ex: quarter).
- Review weekly (see [[Weekly KR Check-in]]).
- Keep a one-page view (see [[OKR One-Pager]]).

- Covers: OKR basics, how to write good KRs, and a review cadence.
- Does not cover: Compensation-linked OKR systems or org-wide governance.
