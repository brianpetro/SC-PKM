# Launch Readiness Review

Launch readiness reviews combine evaluation results, customer evidence, operational risk, and support preparation. The review should make a decision legible: ship, hold, limit rollout, or gather more evidence.

For AI features, readiness is not a single score. A model can pass average-quality checks while still failing in high-impact cases. Review notes should therefore include known failure modes, mitigation plans, monitoring metrics, and owner assignments.

The best readiness reviews link directly to evaluation protocols, incident reviews, and customer signal triage. This lets future teams understand why a launch was approved and what conditions would require a rollback or follow-up review.
